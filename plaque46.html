James Cromar Watt (1862 - 1940)

71 Dee Street

Central Aberdeen

Architect, Enameller and Botanist. Born in Aberdeen and educated at Aberdeen Grammar School, he was articled to an Aberdeen architects firm and later awarded the Royal Institute of British Architects gold medal. He is remembered mainly as a jeweller who used gold granulation and foiled enamelling. Watt exhibited widely in Scotland and abroad and was awarded an honorary LLD from Aberdeen University in 1937.
