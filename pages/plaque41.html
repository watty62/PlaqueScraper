Lilias Gillespie Skene (1626 - 1697)

Religious Society of Friends Meeting House, Crown Street

Central Aberdeen

Quaker Poet. She joined Aberdeens Quaker community in 1666 at a time when the sect was subject to prolonged persecution and her husband, Baillie Alexander Skene of Newtyle, was imprisoned in the Tolbooth for his religious beliefs. The author of a volume of poems and godly ballads entitled On Growing Tryalls, she also wrote several other poems relating to the history of the Quakers in the period 1668-1681. She is buried in the Quaker cemetery at Kingswells.
