John Phillip (1817 - 1867)

21 Skene Square

Central Aberdeen

Artist. Born at 13 Skene Square. He began his career reputedly as a house painter and glazier. In 1837 he moved to London where he entered the Royal Academy Schools. He was a member of an artistic group called the 'Clique'. He became an honorary Royal Scottish Academician in 1857 and a Royal Academician in 1859. Throughout his career, Phillip painted Scottish genre scenes, in the tradition of Sir David Wilkie. From 1851, a series of visits to Spain inspired him to paint colourful and vibrant scenes of Spanish life. Queen Victoria particularly favoured his work and he enjoyed many Royal commissions.
