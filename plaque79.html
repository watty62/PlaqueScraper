James Clerk Maxwell (1831 - 1879)

131 Union Street, Back Wynd Steps

Central Aberdeen

Natural Philosopher. Born at 14 India Street Edinburgh, where the James Clerk Maxwell Foundation premises are now situated, and educated at Edinburgh Academy and Edinburgh and Cambridge Universities he contributed to the analysis of colour perception. He became the first person to project a colour photograph, but he is best known for his work on electricity and magnetism. Maxwell served as Professor of Natural Philosophy at Marischal College and London University.
